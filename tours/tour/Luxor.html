Description
  • Embark on an extraordinary journey to Luxor, the world's greatest open-air museum and ancient Egypt's most spectacular archaeological destination,
  • departing directly from your Hurghada hotel in a luxury air-conditioned coach.
  • The scenic desert drive takes approximately 17 hours through Egypt's Eastern Desert landscape with a comfortable rest stop for refreshments.
  • Your comprehensive Luxor exploration begins on the East Bank with a visit to the magnificent Karnak Temple Complex,
  • the largest religious building ever constructed in human history, which took over 2000 years to complete across multiple pharaonic dynasties.
  • You'll wander among towering stone columns and through the breathtaking Great Hypostyle Hall containing 134 massive pillars,
  • while listening to detailed explanations about ancient Egyptian gods, religious ceremonies, and pharaonic building techniques from your expert Egyptology guide.
  • Continue to the stunning Luxor Temple, connected to Karnak by the ancient Sacred Avenue of Sphinxes,
  • followed by a delicious traditional Egyptian lunch at a restaurant overlooking the legendary Nile River with panoramic views.
  • On the West Bank, you'll choose between visiting the Valley of the Kings (with €10 supplement) where legendary pharaohs including Tutankhamun and Ramesses II were buried,
  • or the Valley of the Queens (included in base price) featuring beautiful royal women's burial chambers with exceptional artistic preservation and fewer crowds.
  • Your archaeological adventure concludes with Queen Hatshepsut's unique rock-cut mortuary temple, considered one of ancient Egypt's greatest architectural achievements,
  • and the massive Colossi of Memnon statues that have stood guard over ancient Thebes for over 3400 years.
  • Return to Hurghada in the evening with unforgettable memories of pharaonic Egypt's golden age.
Included
  • Round-trip luxury air-conditioned coach transportation from all Hurghada hotel areas
  • All entrance fees to temples and archaeological sites mentioned in the standard itinerary

  • Traditional Egyptian lunch at Nile-view restaurant with authentic local cuisine

Not Included
  • Valley of the Kings entrance fees (€10 supplement per person for tomb access)
  • Beverages and drinks during meals at restaurants and rest stops
  • Special tomb entrances including Tutankhamun or Ramesses VI (additional fees required)
  • Horse carriage rides or shopping excursions in local bazaars and markets
  • Personal shopping, souvenirs, and handicraft purchases throughout the tour
  • Photography tickets required for camera use inside tomb chambers (additional fees)
  • Gratuities for tour guide and driver (recommended but optional)
What to Bring
  • Comfortable cotton clothing that is modest and respectful (covering shoulders and knees required)
  • Comfortable walking shoes suitable for uneven ancient stone surfaces and sandy areas
  • Wide-brimmed hat, quality sunglasses, and high SPF sunscreen protection (minimum SPF 50)
  • Digital camera with extra batteries and memory cards for photography opportunities
  • 3-4 liters of water per person (absolutely essential due to long day and desert heat)
What Not to Bring
  • Perishable food items that may spoil in desert heat conditions
  • Large or heavy bags that will be cumbersome during temple visits
  • Expensive jewelry or large amounts of cash for security reasons
Pro Tips
  • Be prepared for punctual departure at the exact scheduled pickup time from your hotel
  • Dress modestly and respectfully when visiting sacred ancient Egyptian religious sites
  • Carry sufficient water (3-4 liters per person) as only one bottle is provided during lunch service
  • Decide between Valley of Kings or Queens options at booking time or discuss with guide during tour
  • Listen carefully to your Egyptology guide's explanations to maximize your learning experience and historical understanding
  • Avoid touching ancient walls, hieroglyphs, and archaeological artifacts for preservation reasons
  • Keep all entrance tickets with you throughout the entire tour for re-entry purposes
